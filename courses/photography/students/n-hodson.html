Natalie Hodson

About Natalie's Work

Inevitably they find their way into the family. It is there that they lose and find themselves. It is there that they gain a sense of what is to be done. The family is always large, immense, great, and mysterious. No one ever gains power over the family, but the family possesses the power to change lives and alter destinies. In many ways it is the supreme authority on earth and often the great provider. It is not only Hansel and Gretel who get lost in the family and then return wiser and fulfilled. - Rewording of ‘the Brothers Grimm: From Enchanted Forests to the Modern World, Second Edition’ By Jack Zipes (2002)

My grandmother’s photography aroused my sensory knowledge of a forgotten childhood landscape. The images captured on her honeymoon formed a visual response that made visible the undercurrents of our family relationship. An awareness of this memory produced a new, imagined landscape that depicted the dark forest of my childhood against the beauty of the Swiss Alps.
