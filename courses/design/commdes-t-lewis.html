About Tifanie

My genuine interest lies in portraiture. There is something tangible and intangible in the human face. It is a place where we reveal or hide ourselves. I am fascinated by this universal image of humanity and especially interested in social documentary where I endeavour to offer a true and unambiguous representation of my subjects.

On a personal note, my final major pictures reflect my compassion for the vulnerable. At 3 years, my younger brother was diagnosed with Autistic Spectrum Disorder. As a result, my sensitivity grew towards those with special needs. For me, photography facilitates my vision to commemorate those who are overlooked - celebrating difference and promoting equality. As a form of visual literacy, photography has enabled me to inform, educate and reveal the uniqueness and similarities of individuals.

Character revelation, tonality, colour and composition are defining facets of my photographic style. I enjoy piecing together snippets of reality to create a visual narrative. Correspondingly, photo-manipulation was kept to a minimum, congruous with my documentary approach – realistic documenting.
