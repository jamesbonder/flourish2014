Verity Mallett

BA (Hons) Fashion Promotion

About Verity

Window displays help the customer make an initial decision about where they shop as they visually interpret a brands values and ethos through first appearances alone. For my final major project I wanted to explore the ways in which an international brands values and ethos could be visually communicated to the British consumer through a window display. I chose to place Australian clothing label Evil Twin into Selfridges flagship store as I felt both brands complimented the quirky and experimental natures of each other.

As the fashion industry has moved in parallel with advancements in technology I wanted to explore how a digital window could be used to replace traditional product. I chose create a film as I felt this would strongly communicate the brands ethos and values and be visually stimulating to the audience and keep their attention. After thorough brand research I identified key traits of the Selfridges customer and created a concept which would visually communicate a message which could be interpreted regardless of gender, nationality or culture. The promotional item runs in parallel with the window display using film but still follows the typical conventions of Selfridges promotion.

The concept was based on the Grimm's fairytales which was inspired by evil twins dark, edgy attitude and current trend of mirrors used in fashion imagery. The project meets the customer needs of both brands and all the elements accurately translate the brand values of up and coming brand Evil Twin.
